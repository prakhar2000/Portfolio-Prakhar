Four years shipping identity, messaging & regulatory systems at global scale — now headed into AI research.

I spent the last four years as a Software Development Engineer at BlackRock, building the infrastructure that Aladdin and its 1M+ users rely on — identity systems, messaging gateways, regulatory pipelines, and, more recently, the agentic layer that lets natural language talk directly to internal APIs.

My work sat at the intersection of distributed backend systems and applied ML: Model Context Protocol servers, LangChain-based RAG pipelines, and gRPC services that needed to be as reliable as they were intelligent. Alongside BlackRock, I've published research on lip-reading systems and IoT safety devices, and I keep building smaller AI tools on the side.

I've now moved to Los Angeles to start a Master's in Computer Science (AI) at the University of Southern California — doubling down on the ML side of the stack.
